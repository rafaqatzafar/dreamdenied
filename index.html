The Policy

What Is Happening In Georgia?

Under Georgia Senate Bill 492, signed in 2008, undocumented students — including DACA recipients raised entirely in this state — are required to pay out-of-state tuition rates three to four times higher than their classmates, for the exact same Georgia education.

Board of Regents Policy 4.1.6, passed in 2010, completely bans undocumented students from enrolling at Georgia's most prestigious public universities, including the University of Georgia and Georgia Tech. They cannot even apply.

On top of this, these students are denied all state financial aid, regardless of their academic achievement or how long they have lived in Georgia.

Rafael Aragon grew up in Georgia. He moved to Connecticut for college because Georgia's out-of-state tuition was unaffordable. His engineering and computer science classmates — who also grew up in Georgia — never came back. Georgia spent 13 years educating them and handed their futures to another state.

This is not one story. This is happening to 13,000 Georgians right now.
